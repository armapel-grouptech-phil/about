About Armapel

Armapel is a dynamic technology company and web developer at the forefront of the Philippines' digital transformation. The company's identity is deeply rooted in the vision of its founder, Arbie Mapalo Peligro, whose name forms the very essence of Armapel (Ar-Ma-Pel). This signifies a personal commitment to excellence in every project, from enterprise-level solutions to public-facing platforms.

Corporate History

November 2024

The company was founded with the launch of its initial product, lingk IPTV. This service provided users with an M3U link hosted on GitHub, aggregating television channels from the Philippines and a selection of global channels from IPTV-org/IPTV. The M3U link was designed for use with third-party IPTV player applications on various devices, including televisions, laptops, and mobile phones.

December 2024

Service for the M3U link was suspended at the end of the month. This decision was made after a key channel from the Philippines became unavailable and a technical resolution could not be readily identified.

February 2025

The company resumed operations with the launch of a new web-based platform, Lingk. This service offered streaming of Cignal Play channels, targeting viewers in the Philippines. The platform was designed to be accessible on-demand across multiple devices for users with a stable internet connection.

April 2025

A significant corporate rebranding was undertaken. The company name was changed from lingk IPTV Inc. to Flow Streaming Co., Ltd. and the Lingk website was rebranded as Flow Later that month, on April 25, the service was enhanced and renamed Flow+ incorporating a library of trending television shows and movies through a content partnership with The Movie Database (TMDB). A secondary product, FlowNews was launched on April 29 in collaboration with News API to provide global news content.

May 2025

FlowNews was discontinued on May 20 due to persistent server-side technical issues. Subsequently, the partnership with TMDB for Flow+ was terminated due to financial constraints, leading to the removal of the trending shows and movies feature. The Flow+ website was then taken offline.

July 2025

The company rebranded for a second time, changing its name from Flow Streaming Co., Ltd. to Armapel. The first initiative under the new corporate identity was the launch of Uzer! a username generator tool, on July 9. On July 15, the company relaunched its core streaming service, rebranding the former Flow+ as Liveplay. This revamped platform aggregated content from multiple sources, including Cignal Play, a curated selection of channels from IPTV-org/IPTV, YouTube live streams, and iWant.

August 2025

On August 10, the Lingk brand was reintroduced with the launch of a new global website. This platform now hosts the M3U link for international channels sourced from iptv-org/iptv on GitHub.
